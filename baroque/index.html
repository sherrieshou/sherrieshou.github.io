Gerard ter Borch is proficient with creating portraits and genre paintings of introverted and quiet dark tone scenes. At the same time, he likes to paint objects that imply wealth in the paintings, such as well decorated furniture and gorgeous fabrics of various materials. In most of Gerard ter Borch's paintings, the characters stand in an empty dark space, allowing the audience to focus on their expressions and costumes. As Angela K. Ho stated in the book, Creating Distinctions in Dutch Genre Painting: Repetition and Invention, "The spare background helps focus the viewer's gaze on the triangular group of foreground figures, with Ter Borch not only rendering their facial expressions and body language with care, but also lavishing attention on their costumes."
